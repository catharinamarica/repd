How It Works
What is the Readiness Score?
Your Readiness Score tells you how prepared your body is for training today. It combines objective data from your training history with your subjective input to generate a personalized training recommendation.
The 4 Factors
Recovery (40%) — Calculated automatically from your recent training volume. High volume in the last few days lowers recovery. Based on the Fitness-Fatigue Model (Bannister, 1975): every session produces both a long-term fitness effect and short-term fatigue. Fatigue decays faster (~15 days) than fitness (~45 days).

Feel (35%) — Your subjective energy level right now. Tired, okay, or strong? This captures what numbers can't: stress, motivation, soreness perception.

Sleep (25%) — How much you slept last night. Sleep is the #1 recovery factor — poor sleep directly impacts strength, coordination, and injury risk.
Score = Recovery×0.4 + Feel×0.35 + Sleep×0.25
Muscle Recovery
On top of the score, Repd tracks which muscles you trained and how long ago. Muscles are marked as Sore (<24h), Recovering (1–2 days), Almost Ready (2–3 days), or Ready (3+ days). This is used to recommend which workout day fits best — avoiding sore muscles while hitting recovered ones.
The Recommendation
Your training recommendation combines all of the above:
🔥 Score 80+ → Go all out, PR day
✅ Score 60–79 → Train as planned
⚡ Score 40–59 → Train lighter, good form
🛋 Score <40 → Rest or active recovery
When muscles are sore, the recommendation also suggests which workout day has the least overlap — so you always train what's ready.
